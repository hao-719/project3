My Favorite City: Tainan

Ancient temples, rich history, traditional street food, and vibrant night markets.

Why Tainan?

Tainan is a historic city where tradition and modern life blend together. Known for its ancient temples, rich cultural heritage, and incredible street food, it offers something for everyone, from peaceful heritage sites to lively night markets filled with energy. Tainan is especially meaningful to me because it is where I was born and grew up, and where I attended high school and college. The city holds many of my memories, and its slower pace, warm atmosphere, and deep history make it a place I will always feel connected to.

Anping Old Fort (安平古堡)

Anping Old Fort is one of Tainan’s most famous historic landmarks. Built by the Dutch in the 17th century, it reflects Taiwan’s early international history. Visitors can explore the fortress walls, enjoy views from the top, and experience traditional streets and local snacks nearby.

Learn more about Anping Old Fort

Confucius Temple (孔廟)

Tainan Confucius Temple is one of the city’s most important cultural landmarks. Built in 1665, it is the first Confucius Temple in Taiwan and is often called the birthplace of education on the island. Visitors can walk through its peaceful courtyards and traditional architecture while experiencing Tainan’s deep historical roots.

Plan your visit to The Confucius Temple

Chimei Museum (奇美博物館)

Chimei Museum is one of Tainan’s most impressive cultural attractions. Known for its European-style architecture, it houses collections of art, musical instruments, and historical artifacts from around the world. The grand plaza and surrounding park also make it a beautiful place to visit and relax.

Check out the newest exhibitions at Chimei Museum

Flowe Night Market (花園夜市)

Flower Night Market is one of Tainan’s most popular night markets. Known for its wide variety of street food, games, and local vendors, it captures the lively energy of the city at night. It is the perfect place to try classic Taiwanese snacks and experience Tainan’s vibrant food culture.

Visit Flower Night Market

Quick Facts

  • Population: About 1.85 million
  • Languages: Mandarin Chinese and Taiwanese
  • Currency: New Taiwan Dollar (TWD)
  • Climate: Tropical monsoon climate with hot, humid summers and mild winters
  • Transport: Trains, buses, scooters, and high-speed rail connections to other major cities
